Today marks the first time I have ever written a blog. And whilst I may not be very good at it, I feel that the writing will help me put in writing my perspectives on leadership, communications, management and other experiences that I may have gathered over the years and, through this process, readers may also gain insights that can help them navigate the challenges as they pursue their own leadership journey. 

Why is this post titled **"Candid Musings from a Casual Leader"**? Well, this blog is not about observations backed by profound academic research, but frank and personal reflections and realizations or my **"candid musings",** potentially peppered with personal anecdotes or wisdom that has been shared to me by those who are wiser and better than myself. 

To be honest, I have never deliberately sought out leadership roles or even really wanted to push myself along the corporate ladder. But more often than not I happened to be there at the right place, at the right time, to fall headfirst into problems. The fact that I survived these falls means I survived to fight another battle another day but am better armed with the wisdom gained through failure or from the many "mentors" I have unofficially adopted. So on that basis, I deem myself a **"leader by circumstance"**.

To me, Leader is a high title that comes with tons of challenges and burdens that some aspire towards without proper understanding of the responsibilities that it entails. As a good friend once told me, **"If service is beneath you, leading is beyond you"**. A somber reminder that leaders have to bear the burden of trust, care, guidance, motivation and many others for their follows and the ultimate reward is but only a 6 letter title.  

On that note, I thank you for taking the time to read my humble musings and I hope you continue to support my writing simply through your reading.
